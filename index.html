Featured Authors

Four extraordinary voices sharing stories that linger long after the last page.

Fiction · 7:10 PM

Maya Chen

"The Weight of Forgotten Seasons" (2024)

Maya Chen's debut novel has been called "a lantern held up to the fog of grief." A former librarian from Portland, she writes about immigrant families, memory, and the quiet courage it takes to begin again.

Winner of the Pacific Northwest Book Award, Maya will read from her bestselling debut and answer audience questions. Her work has been translated into 14 languages and praised by The New York Times as "essential reading for our times."

Poetry & Essays · 8:15 PM

James Okafor

"Letters to a City I Never Left" (2023)

James Okafor writes with the precise tenderness of someone who has learned to love a place by almost losing it. His essays on Black urban life in America have appeared in The Atlantic and Orion Magazine.

His reading will include unpublished poems from his forthcoming collection due in fall 2025. James has been awarded the Oregon Book Award and is a two-time finalist for the National Book Critics Circle Award.

Classic Romance · 8:20 PM

Jane Austen

"Pride and Prejudice" (1813)

Renowned for her witty portrayal of 19th-century English society, Jane Austen's novels remain as sharp and relevant as ever. Her keen observations on class, marriage, and morality made her one of the most beloved writers in the English language.

A special dramatic reading of Pride and Prejudice will open this session, performed by members of the Portland Literary Theatre. This tribute evening celebrates Austen's enduring legacy and her revolutionary voice in fiction.

Inspirational Fiction · 9:00 PM

Paulo Coelho

"The Alchemist" (1988)

Author of The Alchemist — one of the best-selling books in history — Coelho writes deeply spiritual and motivational stories about life, destiny, and purpose.

His closing reading will be followed by a book-signing session for the first 30 attendees. Coelho's works have been translated into 88 languages and sold over 320 million copies worldwide, making him one of the most widely read authors of all time.
